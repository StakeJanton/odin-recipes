Shepherds Pie

Shepherds Pie is one of my favorite dishes. It is super tasty and super filling. Most of the time me and my wife actually make Cottage Pie, which is with ground beef instead of lamb, but the recipe is basically the same and you can sub in any protein that you want.

Ingredients:

  • 3 large potatoes
  • 1/4 cup milk
  • 2 tablespoon butter
  • 1 egg
  • salt & pepper
  • 2 tablespoons canola oil
  • 1 medium onion
  • 1 to 2 cups mix veggies (diced carrots, corn, pees, etc.)
  • 1 1/2 pounds ground beef
  • 1 cup of broth
  • 1 tablespoon Worcestershire sauce
  • 1 cup shredded sharp cheder cheese

Directions:

  1. Dice the potatoes and place in pot of water. Set on high heat and cover until boiling. Once boiling, remove lid and reduce heat to a simmer until potatoes are tender. Then drain the potatoes and return to pot. Mash potatoes and add milk, butter, salt & pepper until smooth. Stir in the egg until well combined.
  2. As the oven is preheating and the potatoes are boiling, you can prepare the filling. Put the oil in a saute pan and set over medium heat. Once this is hot add the onion and mixed veggies for about 3-4 min. Then add the ground beef with salt & pepper until browned and cooked through. Add the broth and Worcestershire sauce and stir until combined. bring to a boil and then reduce heat to low allowing the filling to cook for about 10-12 minutes or until the sauce has thickened.
  3. In a 11 by 7 glass dish, add the filling to the bottom then top with mashed potatoes. Make sure to cover around the edges to create a seal to prevent the filling from bubbling up. Smooth mashed potatoes with spatula then top with cheese.
  4. Set oven to Broil then put Shepherds Pie in for about 5 minutes or until cheese just starts to brown. Allow it to cool before serving.
